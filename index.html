01

The moat

The computer-vision components VICKI uses — detection, tracking, pose, shot classification — are scientific commodity. Anyone with the right skills can assemble a comparable pipeline in 6–12 months. Competing there means competing on price and UI polish, where incumbents already have scale.

VICKI's advantage sits elsewhere: temporal layering. One shot analyzed today is worth little. Ten thousand shots of an athlete over eighteen months — correlated with the coach's methodological decisions, match outcomes, the athlete's physical response, the corrections that worked and the ones that didn't — is an asset you don't replicate by buying better infrastructure. It only accrues with time. The moat typically matures after 6–12 months of continuous use.

Results are obvious; growth is often hidden — VICKI proves it. Match results are a lagging, noisy signal: an athlete can improve their backhand, movement and tactics and still lose, or get worse on paper while rebuilding a stroke. VICKI sees the real progress the scoreboard hides. Access to academies and clubs is a significant family investment, and it has to feel well spent: that objective proof of growth — even in a flat-results season — is exactly what keeps a family from walking away. It's the difference between a thermometer and a diagnosis.

Established analytics players record measurable facts on court — shot speed, bounce position, patterns, match stats. Their history is a history of measurements. That's real value. But it's a different layer.

These layers don't compete — they integrate. And the difference isn't replicable by adding features: even a player who started building a methodological layer today would start from zero memory, and layered memory doesn't synthesize. It accumulates.

Why an academy buys it — the ROI

An ELITE court runs €240/month on top of the site base — the recurring fee covers cloud analysis, maintenance, software updates and the full methodological layer (hardware is one-off, separate). A modest cost against the value it unlocks. It pays for itself through several compounding levers:

Premium pricing

A VICKI®-powered academy — measurable results, tracked method — justifies raising fees by a few points across its existing athletes. That alone covers the subscription.

Attendance & uniqueness

VICKI® makes the academy unique: the one that proves growth with objective data attracts athletes the others can't intercept. New enrolments the competition doesn't see.

Retention

The athlete's journey lives inside the academy — leaving means starting from zero memory. Higher switching cost, lower churn, recurring fees secured year over year.

On top of these, every advantage the platform already delivers: the coach's method codified as an asset, the academy's heritage as institutional memory, continuity even when a coach leaves, transparency for families. VICKI® is an investment for the academy, not a cost — and the customer's success compounds VICKI®'s moat: the more the academy grows athletes, the richer the methodological memory it can't take elsewhere.

Three levers of scale

Because the value is the methodological layer, not the vision itself, VICKI is agnostic to the analysis source. That opens three complementary go-to-market modes.

Mode 01

Integrated

End-to-end: VICKI's own vision pipeline plus the methodological layer. For new academies with no installed analytics.

Full margin · linear growth, one sale at a time
Mode 02

Overlay

The academy keeps its existing analytics infrastructure; VICKI sits on top and adds the methodological brain it doesn't have. Additive pitch, low adoption friction.

High margin · no hardware cost
Mode 03

White-label

VICKI embedded as the methodological layer inside an analytics player's product, reaching their entire installed base through one channel.

Distribution at scale · CAC drops by an order of magnitude

The structural defense in any white-label deal: the layered memory accumulated stays with VICKI and the end clients, not the partner. A player who replaced VICKI would start from zero memory — losing the methodological continuity their clients built. The moat protects the channel.

Pricing & unit economics

Recurring software subscription, with hardware billed separately as a one-off so the monthly fee stays software margin — that's how €149/month holds. The methodological layer (the moat) is in every paid tier, not an upsell.

TierPrice / monthWhat it is
BASE€149 per coachVia iPad — no fixed hardware. The adoption engine, near-pure margin.
PRO€390 + €200base + per court4 cameras/court. Reduced domains (no medical/mental, basic biomechanics) + the methodological layer. E.g. 1 court €590, 2 courts €790.
ELITE€490 + €240base + per court6 cameras/court. Full domain depth + complete methodological layer. E.g. 1 court €730, 2 courts €970, 3 courts €1,210.
OVERLAY€690 per courtOn third-party cameras — VICKI adds the methodological brain. Edge only, no cameras.
WHITE-LABELper partnerEmbedded in an analytics player's product. ~€75k integration + ~€20/court/month. 8+ courts → enterprise, negotiated.

A base fee per site + a fee per court. The base covers the platform, the methodological layer and the shared edge (per-site costs); the per-court fee covers cameras and analysis (per-court costs). So a one-court academy pays for one court, not for three — the price scales with what they use.

Hardware, two options the customer chooses from. A — One-off setup: €2,500 edge per site (shared across adjacent courts) + €4,118/court PRO / €5,738/court ELITE, then the subscription. E.g. ELITE 1 court: €8,238 + €730/month. B — All-inclusive: no upfront, hardware spread into the monthly fee over 36 months. E.g. ELITE 1 court: €977/month all-in. The edge runs local pre-analysis and sends data — not video — to cloud processing, so one edge serves several courts.

The all-inclusive option shifts revenue from one-off to recurring — it lifts ARR but requires working capital, since VICKI fronts the hardware and recovers it through the fee.

How €149/month holds

Cameras cost €600 each, the edge ~€1,750. Separating a one-off setup from the recurring fee removes hardware amortisation from the monthly price: BASE rides an iPad (no rig), PRO/ELITE recover hardware up front via setup — so the monthly fee covers cloud, maintenance and margin, not the rig.
